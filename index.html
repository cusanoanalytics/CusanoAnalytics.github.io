Massachusetts Risk Adjustment Transfers — 2024 Benefit Year

Healthcare Economics June 2026

How $238.8M moved through Massachusetts' ACA risk adjustment program in 2024. Features the redistribution of dollars from insurers with healthier-than-average enrollees to insurers carrying a sicker, costlier population. Data collected from cms.gov


Bars extending left (gray) paid into the risk pool; their enrollees were healthier/cheaper than the state average. Bars extending right (black) received payments; their enrollees were sicker/costlier than average. The purpose of this diverging bar chart is to show who's paying, and who's getting paid, as a result of risk adjustment transfers.
Are big receivers also carrying catastrophic cost members? The High-Cost Risk Pool separately reimburses 60% of any single enrollee's costs above $1 million. Dots high on both axes are issuers receiving large general risk transfers and carrying genuinely catastrophic outlier cases.
In the Risk Adjustment Waterfall Chart, each bar represents one issuer's transfer amount, stacked onto the running total of everyone before it. Black bars climb the total (receivers) and gray bars bring it back down (payers). The waterfall shows how $238.8M moves through Massachusett's risk pool, and zeroes out from issuers with the most expensive members to the least expensive members.
Data: CMS Summary Report on Individual and Small Group Market Risk Adjustment Transfers, 2024 Benefit Year
